Biryani

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 500g chicken (cut into pieces)
  • 2 onions (sliced)
  • 1 tomato (chopped)
  • 2-3 green chilies (slit lengthwise)
  • 1/4 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 cup chopped coriander leaves (cilantro)
  • 1/2 cup chopped mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup cooking oil or ghee (clarified butter)
  • Whole spices (2 bay leaves, 4-5 cloves, 4-5 green cardamom pods, 2-inch cinnamon stick)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon biryani masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Saffron strands soaked in warm milk (for garnish, optional)

Procedure:

  1. Marinate the Chicken:
    • In a bowl, mix the chicken pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, chopped coriander leaves, chopped mint leaves, green chilies, turmeric powder, biryani masala, and salt. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Prepare the Rice:
    • Wash the basmati rice thoroughly until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for 30 minutes.
    • In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add salt and the soaked rice. Cook the rice until it is 70-80% cooked. Drain the water and set aside.
  3. Cook the Chicken:
    • Heat oil or ghee in a large pot. Add the sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
    • Add the whole spices and cumin seeds. Sauté for a minute until fragrant.
    • Add the marinated chicken pieces along with chopped tomatoes. Cook until the chicken is tender and cooked through. Adjust salt and spices according to taste.
  4. Layering:
    • In a separate heavy-bottomed pot or a deep baking dish, layer half of the cooked rice.
    • Spread the cooked chicken mixture evenly over the rice layer.
    • Top with the remaining rice, spreading it evenly.
  5. Dum Cooking:
    • Sprinkle saffron-soaked milk over the top layer of rice (if using).
    • Cover the pot tightly with a lid or aluminum foil to seal the steam.
    • Cook on low heat (dum) for 15-20 minutes or until the rice is fully cooked and aromatic.
  6. Serve:
    • Once done, gently fluff the rice layers with a fork.
    • Serve hot chicken biryani garnished with fried onions, chopped coriander leaves, and mint leaves.
    • Enjoy your delicious homemade chicken biryani with raita (yogurt dip) or salad on the side.
