Pav Bhaji

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 cup cauliflower florets, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup green peas
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 bell pepper (capsicum), finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust according to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tablespoons Pav Bhaji masala
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped for garnish
  • 8 pav (bread rolls)
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Procedure:

  1. Heat a large pan or kadai over medium heat and melt the butter.
  2. Add chopped onions and green chilies. Sauté until onions turn translucent.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another minute until the raw aroma disappears.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy and oil starts to separate.
  5. Add chopped bell pepper, cauliflower florets, and green peas. Mix well.
  6. Add Pav Bhaji masala, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix until the vegetables are well
    7. coated with the spices.
  7. Mash the vegetables using a potato masher or the back of a spoon. Mash until you achieve a coarse
    8. texture. Add a little water if necessary to adjust the consistency.
  8. Add the mashed potatoes and mix well with the vegetable mixture.
  9. Cook the Pav Bhaji for another 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it reaches the desired
    10. consistency.
  10. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and a dollop of butter.
  11. Toast the pav (bread rolls) on a griddle with a little butter until they are lightly browned and crispy
    12. on the edges.
  12. Serve the hot Pav Bhaji with toasted pav, lemon wedges, and additional chopped onions on the side.
