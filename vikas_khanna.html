Vikas Khanna

Vikas Khanna is a highly acclaimed Indian chef, restaurateur, cookbook author, humanitarian, and television personality, renowned for his innovative approach to Indian cuisine and his efforts to promote culinary arts globally. Born on November 14, 1971, in Amritsar, India, Khanna's culinary journey began at a young age, inspired by his grandmother's traditional Indian cooking.

Khanna pursued formal culinary training at the Welcomgroup Graduate School of Hotel Administration in Manipal, Karnataka. He further honed his skills by working in various prestigious hotels and restaurants in India and abroad, gaining invaluable experience and expertise in diverse culinary traditions.

In 2000, Vikas Khanna moved to the United States to pursue opportunities in the culinary world. He quickly made a name for himself with his exceptional talent and creativity, earning recognition as one of the top chefs in New York City. Khanna's culinary style seamlessly blends traditional Indian flavors with modern techniques, resulting in innovative and visually stunning dishes.

Khanna's culinary achievements extend beyond the kitchen. He is the author of several acclaimed cookbooks, including "Flavors First," "Indian Harvest," and "Khanna Sutra," which have garnered widespread praise for their accessible recipes and insightful culinary tips. His books aim to showcase the rich diversity of Indian cuisine and inspire readers to explore new flavors and cooking techniques.

In addition to his culinary endeavors, Vikas Khanna is deeply committed to humanitarian work. He has been actively involved in various philanthropic initiatives, including serving as a goodwill ambassador for the Smile Foundation, an NGO dedicated to supporting underprivileged children in India. Khanna's efforts to combat hunger and promote education have earned him admiration and respect worldwide.

Khanna's talent and contributions to the culinary world have been recognized with numerous awards and honors, including the Michelin Star for his restaurant Junoon in New York City. He has also been featured on prestigious lists such as the "100 Most Influential People in the World" by Time magazine and the "30 Most Influential Global Indian" by Forbes.

Throughout his career, Vikas Khanna has remained dedicated to celebrating the richness of Indian cuisine, fostering cultural exchange through food, and using his platform to make a positive impact on society. His passion, creativity, and philanthropic spirit continue to inspire aspiring chefs and food enthusiasts around the globe.
