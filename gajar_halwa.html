Gajar Halwa

Ingredients:

  • 500 grams carrots (about 4-5 medium-sized carrots), peeled and grated
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1/4 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • A pinch of saffron strands (optional)
  • Chopped nuts (such as almonds or pistachios) for garnish

Procedure:

  1. Grate the Carrots:
    • Peel the carrots and grate them using a grater. You can also use a food processor for quick shredding.
  2. Cook the Carrots:
    • Heat ghee in a heavy-bottomed pan or kadhai over medium heat.
    • Add the grated carrots to the pan and sauté them in ghee for 5-7 minutes until they turn slightly soft.
  3. Add Milk:
    • Pour the whole milk into the pan with the grated carrots.
    • Stir well and let it come to a simmer.
  4. Simmer:
    • Reduce the heat to low and let the carrots cook in milk until the milk reduces and thickens, and the carrots become soft. This can take about 30-40 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking.
  5. Add Sugar and Flavorings:
    • Once the carrots are cooked and the milk has thickened, add sugar to the mixture.
    • Also, add cardamom powder and saffron strands (if using) for flavor. Stir well to combine.
  6. Continue Cooking:
    • Cook the mixture for another 10-15 minutes until the sugar is fully dissolved, and the halwa reaches a thick consistency. Keep stirring occasionally.
  7. Garnish and Serve:
    • Once the gajar halwa is ready, garnish it with chopped nuts of your choice (such as almonds or pistachios).
    • Serve the gajar halwa warm or cold, according to your preference.
