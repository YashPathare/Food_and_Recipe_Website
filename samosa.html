Samosa

Ingredients:

  • For the dough:
    • 2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt
    • Water (as needed)
  • For the filling:
    • 2 medium potatoes, boiled, peeled, and mashed
    • 1/2 cup green peas, boiled
    • 1 small onion, finely chopped
    • 1 green chili, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
    • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
    • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
    • Salt to taste
    • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

1. Prepare the Dough:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, salt, and vegetable oil.
  2. Gradually add water and knead until a smooth and firm dough is formed.
  3. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.

2. Prepare the Filling:

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a pan over medium heat.
  2. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add chopped onions and green chili. Saute until onions turn translucent.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and saut√© for a minute until the raw aroma disappears.
  5. Add boiled and mashed potatoes, boiled green peas, coriander powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt.
  6. Mix well and cook for 3-4 minutes. Remove from heat and let the filling cool.

3. Shape the Samosas:

  1. Divide the dough into small lemon-sized balls.
  2. Roll each ball into a thin oval or circular shape.
  3. Cut each rolled dough in half to form two semi-circles.
  4. Moisten the straight edge of each semi-circle with water.
  5. Fold the semi-circle into a cone shape, sealing the moistened edge.
  6. Fill each cone with the prepared potato-pea filling.
  7. Seal the open edge of the cone by pressing firmly to form a triangle-shaped samosa.

4. Fry the Samosas:

  1. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat.
  2. Once the oil is hot, gently slide the prepared samosas into the oil, ensuring they are not overcrowded.
  3. Fry the samosas until they turn golden brown and crispy on all sides, turning occasionally.
  4. Remove the fried samosas using a slotted spoon and drain excess oil on paper towels.

5. Serve:

  1. Serve the hot and crispy samosas with mint chutney, tamarind chutney, or tomato ketchup.
